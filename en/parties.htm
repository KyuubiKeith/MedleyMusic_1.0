Battle of the Bands

This tradition is inspired by the band battle between Benny Goodman “The Kind of Swing” and Chick Webb’s, “The King of Drums” Orchestras at the Savoy Ballroom, Harlem, NYC on 1937 May 11th. 

The first Battle of The Bands took part in 2011 edition between Rhythm Junkies (as a home band) and Handsome Fred’s Orchestra (current Hornsgatan Ramblers). It was a rematch since they have battled one before in Stockholm, Sweden. The final verdict was 1:1. 

The Battles became a tradition of the festival and most of the time it is of a show and way of paying respects to the jazz culture, then a competition. Either way each time it is incredible.

Lecture

Charismatic jazz dance instructors and performers are making a panel talk on the topic of traditional jazz music and dance history.

Come and hear stories about jazz era, legendary old-timers, dancers, musicians, and artists. The panel talk will be full of interesting – and previously unknown to many – details about the inception of the dance culture, its place in history, and how it spread across the world. You'll get to hear some stories that have not been written down!

Everyone is welcome to drop in and scoop closer to the roots of jazz!

Jazz Dance Panel Talk is a part of The International Traditional Jazz Dance & Music Festival – Harlem 2018. 

Lecture is free.

Lindy Trip

This tradition started in 2006, when during the summer Lindyhop.lt crew brought social dancing outside and during one evening traveled a few outside spots to dance. 

Now it’s one of the highlights of the event. Saturday afternoon we gather in the center of Vilnius to dance and spread the love for jazz culture. 

Later on, we march down Gediminas avenue, one of the main streets of Vilnius old town together with live music in spirit of New Orleans marches. 

Lindy Trip ends in Cathedral square with an enormous "Shim Sham".

Rules are easy. For the prelims you will have 3 different tempo songs for each heat. You only dance with your partner. Number of heats will be decided according to the number of contestants. The Judges will pick 6 couples for the finals.

The finalists will be announced during the night. Make sure to double check if you’re in it. The finals will take part at 23:30 ±30 min give or take.

The Finals. Couples will pick a number of their spotlight. There will be a warm up song. A spotlight song with 8x8 each couple twice and an all skate. The judges will choose the winners. More details will be given during the competition. 

May the best dancers be victorious!

The format is called “The King of the Hill”.  Finalists stand in line. The first in the line is the King of the hill and has to “defend” his position. The second dancer in line starts the battle. All finalists can dance as long as they want, but be noted, that most of the time, it’s not the length of the dance, that makes it better, so pace yourself since the competition could take a while. If the the spotlight is taking too long, the organizers  will take responsibility to stop the music or pass the spotlight to the other dancer. After both dancers battle each other, the judges vote by pointing to one or the other dancer, or not voting at all, by saying both were equally good. The winner of the battle stays to defend his position and the loser goes back in the end of the line and waits for his next chance to become the “king of the hill”.
The winner is a dancer that collects the most wins of all finalists. If there is a tie the organisers can stop the competition and make the two finalist battle until “death”.  May the best dancer be victorious!

Harlem Group Photo

Since 2014 we’ve been shooting a festival group picture in an old school way. 

HARLEM group photo is made using Big Format Field Camera from 1920s and analog techniques. The final picture is made using classic black and white negative on silver gelatin baryta photo paper, developed by hands and is one of a kind. Prepare to stand still for five or ten minutes just like a hundred years ago.

Ultimate Best Dancers Competition

It will take part on Saturday “Grand Party” at Legendos Club. Prelims and semi-finals will take part around 20:30 and finals - around 23:30. If you are in the competition, make sure you’re there at 20:00 for prelims, and for finals at least 30 min before it’s scheduled, since there a chance it could be earlier and also, later.

Rules are easy. The prelims and semi-finals are the same as a regular J’n’J competition with different heats and partner changing. Number of heats will be decided according to the number of contestants. The Judges will have to see your number, so make sure you have it. Without your number you can’t participate. Semi-finals will take part right after the prelims. Judges will pick 12 leaders and 12 followers for semi-finals. If you get a sticker, you have to go register again and get a number for semi-finals.

The finalists will be announced during the night. Make sure to double check if you’re in it. The finals will take part at 23:30 ±30 min give or take.

The Finals. 

Followers will pick a number of their leader and leader will pick a number of their spotlight. There will be a warm up song. Spotlights goes like this - the first song will be slow and all couples will do two spotlights each. During the first spotlight dancers can't touch, that means, they will dance solo, in front of each other, inspiring each other. Second spotlight is as a regular couple dance. Same idea with the fast song. That means during two songs each couple will have 4 spotlights (1 slow solo, 1 slow couple, 1 fast solo, 1 fast couple) and two all skates. The judges will choose the winners.  

More details will be given during the competition. 

May the best dancers be victorious!

JAZZ BATTLE COMPETITION

This is a solo American vernacular jazz dance contest. Dancers must include Charleston variations and may also include vernacular jazz movements. 

The format is called “The King of the Hill”.  Finalists stand in line. The first in the line is the King of the hill and has to “defend” his position. The second dancer in line starts the battle.


ULTIMATE BEST DANCERS COMPETITION

This will be a social dance competition open to advanced dancers. Leaders and followers will be randomly drawn to dance together to several songs of varying styles and tempos, but will be judged as individuals in the preliminary rounds. Dancers selected to dance in the finals will be randomly assigned a partner again, but will be judged as a couple as they dance in a spotlight format.

SRICTLY LINDY COMPETITION

This will be a competition open to advanced dancers who compete at a high national/international level. 

Dancers register as couples. 

Although choreographed sequences are not disallowed, the judges will be looking for spontaneity, creativity and connection to the music.
